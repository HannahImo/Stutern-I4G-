Yams originated in Africa, Asia, and the Caribbean. Africans call yams “nyami,”
which is where we get the word “yam.” They are cylindrical and vary in size.
Some of the largest yams have weighed more than 100 pounds and have been several feet long.

Ingredients Measurement
Yam 1 tuber of Yam
Tomato 10 firm tomatoes
Groundnut oil 1 cooking spoon serving
blended crayfish 1 teaspoon
Cooking salt a little more than a pinch (according to your discertion)
A ball of oinion chopped to bits
Fresh red pepper and bell pepper one each

  • Peel the tuber of yam and put to cook with small salt
  • while the tomatoes, onion and pepper are chopped to bits.
  • put a pot on the fire, pour your groundnut oil and let to get hot
  • when hot, add your onion, chopped tomatoes, and pepper and leave to cook for about 10mins.
  • Bring your yam down when ready.
  • Add crayfish and salt to boiling sauce.
  • allow to cook then add your whisked eggs.
  • taste at intervals and bring down when ready.
